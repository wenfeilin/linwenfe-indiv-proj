# Experiment Playbook for Experiment 3

## Research Question

Is playing music from the individual entry and from a month's worth of entries intuitive? Is it clear the limits of each?

## Script

In the calendar view, you have a CD-player-looking music player, and in the entry view, you have an iPod-looking music player. 

Figure out and explain what these music players do, how they differ, and the song-playing limits of each. 


(After the experiment) This is the answer: In the calendar view, you have a CD-player-looking music player that can play the songs from your entries starting from a certain date. It is intended for you to be able to play your songs of the month. In the entry view, you have an iPod-looking music player that is used to search for and select a song to be tied to that specific entry. You can also play this one song on loop when writing your entry. 

## Rules for Interaction

To limit the scope of this experiment, only the components that are colored can be clicked on. You will use a pencil to act as a cursor. When you want to click on something, tap on what you're clicking on. If the click was valid, change will occur on the paper prototype.

## Data To Be Collected

- time spent to complete the task
- notes on any difficulties encountered/unintended actions done?
- a rating of the overall experience plus an explanation of that rating
  - what they liked
  - what they didn't like
  - did they do what they intended to do?
- answers to the research question(s)
- aspects the participant of this experiment would like to change about this design
